how much do you match ?

Love and dating are a huge part of our lives, especially for young people. Whether through dating apps or going out, we spend a lot of time searching for the perfect match. One approach that some people use to seek a potential match is through speed dating. To assist their students in their quest for love, a university arranged a sequence of speed dating gatherings. Here, we examine who attended, their expectations, and the overall success of these events.

Intro Title

Does Speed Dating Work ?
From 2002 to 2004, an American University hosted a series of twenty-one speed dating events that attracted a whopping 400+ students. Extensive data was meticulously collected, capturing details about each participant, their dates, and their preferences. This included age, ethnicity, gender, field of study, desired partner qualities, interests, and self-assessments. The importance participants placed on attractiveness, sincerity, ambition, intelligence, humor, and shared interests was carefully recorded. After each date, both individuals scored their partners and decided on the possibility of a second date. All of this information is presented in the following pages, enjoy !

Before delving into more detailed information, presented here is some basic information about the students who attended the events.

What are people looking for ?

To find a match, individuals express the key traits they seek in a potential partner. To illustrate these preferences, we used a spider chart. To view the preferences of a specific gender, simply click the corresponding button and deselect the others. To observe preferences within a particular age range, adjust the slider accordingly. Notably, the results indicate that women prioritize qualities like sincerity, intelligence, and shared interests. Conversely, men tend to place a greater emphasis on attractiveness compared to other traits.

What are they interested in ?

One crucial aspect that individuals seek in a partner is shared interests, both for dating and everyday life. To represent the average ratings given to these interests, we opted for a bubble chart. Participants provided ratings ranging from 0 to 100, where 0 signifies no interest at all and 100 represents total interest. By selecting the respective buttons, you can view the average interests per gender, ethnicity, or main field of study/work. Additionally, you can choose specific age ranges by checking the corresponding boxes. The bubbles are color-coded and hovering the mouse over them reveals the group names. Notably, there are substantial differences in interests such as gaming and shopping among the various groups. Interestingly, music and movies receive high ratings across all groups.

Who matched ?

To determine the individuals who match the most, we present a sankey diagram with four columns: gender, ethnicity, highest characteristic, and failed/successful date. Note that, a successful date corresponds to a mutual match, and that each individual participates to multiple dates. Hovering the mouse over the links shows the number of sources that are associated to the corresponding target. To enhance clarity and view proportions more distinctly, you can uncheck certain categories to remove them from the sankey diagram. One intriguing finding is that a significant proportion of individuals prioritize sincerity as their highest characteristic. Surprisingly, sincerity has one of the lowest number of successful dates. On the other hand, attractiveness and intelligence are associated with the highest number of successful dates.
